Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose

How Bose Founded an Army to Fight the British

Subhas Chandra Bose was an Indian nationalist who defied British rule in India and sought foreign allies to liberate his country.

Entire life of the Netaji Subash Chandra Bose:

  • Birth :

    1. January 23, 1897: Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose was born in Cuttack, Bengal Presidency (now Odisha, India)

  • Education :

    1. Baptist Mission’s Protestant European School, Cuttack (1902–09).

    2. Ravenshaw Collegiate School, Cuttack (1909–12).

    3. Presidency College, Calcutta (1912–15).

    4. Scottish Church College, Calcutta (1917–1919).

    5. Fitzwilliam Hall, Non-Collegiate Students Board, Cambridge (1919–21).

  • Involvement in the Nationalist Movement :

    1. In 1921, Bose returned to India and joined the nationalist movement led by Mahatma Gandhi and the Indian National Congress.

    2. He aligned with Jawaharlal Nehru’s group within the Congress, which was more open to socialism.

  • Leadership Roles :

    1. In 1938, Bose became the President of the Indian National Congress.

    2. In 1939, He founded the All India Forward Bloc.

    3. In 1943 to 1945, As the leader of the Indian National Army (INA), he fought against British forces in Southeast Asia during World War II.

  • Controversial Alliances :

    1. Bose formed wartime alliances with Nazi Germany and Imperial Japan, which left a legacy marked by authoritarianism, anti-Semitism, and military challenges.

  • Personal details :

    1. Full Name: Subhas Chandra Bose.

    2. Parents:
    Father: Janakinath Bose
    Mother: Prabhabati Bose.

    3. Honorific Title: “Netaji” (Bengali: “Respected Leader”).

    4. Spouse: Emilie Schenkl (secretly married without ceremony or witnesses, unacknowledged publicly by Bose).

    5. Children: Anita Bose Pfaff.

  • Death :

    1. August 18, 1945: Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose died from third-degree burns sustained in an aircrash in Japanese Taiwan (present-day Taipei, Taiwan).

    2. Some Indians initially doubted the crash, hoping for his return to secure India’s independence.

    3. Resting Place: Renkō-ji, Tokyo, Japan.

    • "One individual may die for an idea, but that idea will, after his death, incarnate itself in a thousand lives."

    --Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose
